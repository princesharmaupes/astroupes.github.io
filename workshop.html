About the Workshop

Astronomical data analysis is the cornerstone of modern astrophysics. With the rise of large-scale surveys, space missions, and automated observatories, the ability to handle and analyze data has become an essential skill. This workshop will equip UG/PG students with practical skills in astronomical data handling and data-driven methods.

Students will engage with real datasets (TESS, Gaia, Kepler) and learn techniques such as time-series analysis, photometry, and applications of machine learning in astronomy.

Objectives

  • Introduce fundamentals of astronomical data analysis.
  • Provide hands-on experience with observational datasets.
  • Familiarize participants with Python, Astropy, Lightkurve, Astroquery.
  • Encourage regional collaboration and capacity building.

Tentative Program Schedule

  • Morning Sessions: Lectures by invited experts (topics: photometry, spectroscopy, time-series, ML applications).
  • Afternoon Sessions: Hands-on Python training (Astropy, Lightkurve, Lomb-Scargle, real TESS/Gaia/Kepler data).
  • Evening (Day 1): Telescope observation session (weather permitting).
  • Day 3: Summary discussions, Q&A, valedictory.
