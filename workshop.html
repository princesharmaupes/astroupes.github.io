About the Workshop

The Workshop on Data Science in Astronomy introduces students to modern, data-driven techniques in astronomy through expert lectures and hands-on tutorials using real survey data (TESS, Kepler, Gaia). You'll learn time-series analysis, photometry, and Python workflows with libraries such as Astropy and Lightkurve.

Designed for Undergraduate (2nd & 3rd year) and Postgraduate students in physics/astronomy/data-science.

Organized by UPES (Dehradun) in collaboration with IUCAA (Pune).

Eligibility

  • UG (2nd or 3rd year) students in Physics, Mathematics, Computer Science, or related fields.
  • PG (M.Sc./M.Tech) students in Physics/Astronomy/Data Science.
  • Basic programming familiarity recommended (Python/Jupyter).

Tentative Program Schedule

Format: Morning lectures by invited experts; Afternoon hands-on labs using real data; Telescope session on Day-1 evening (weather permitting).

Date Session Focus
Feb 24 (Day 1) Morning Opening & lectures: observational astronomy; variable stars — photometric/spectroscopic classification
Afternoon Hands-on: Python setup, Astropy & Lightkurve basics; working with TESS/Kepler light curves; Lomb–Scargle periodograms
Evening Telescope observation session at UPES (weather permitting)
Feb 25 (Day 2) Morning Lectures: time-series analysis; machine-learning applications in astrophysics
Afternoon Hands-on: feature extraction, classification demos; Gaia cross-matching & catalog queries
Feb 26 (Day 3) Morning Lectures + practical wrap-up with real case studies
Afternoon Panel/Q&A, feedback, certificates & valedictory

Tentative Schedule. Actual ordering may vary depending on speaker availability.
